## 👋 Who Am I?

I'm **Yash Warulkar**, an AI & Full Stack Engineer, Product Engineer, and Co-Founder of **Innovara Dynamics**, driven by a simple belief:

> **Build products that create real impact, not just projects that demonstrate technical skills.**

I enjoy taking ideas from a blank page to production-ready platforms, combining software engineering, artificial intelligence, cloud technologies, and product thinking to create solutions that solve meaningful real-world problems.

Whether it's architecting enterprise software, developing AI-powered applications, or building complete ecosystems, I enjoy every stage of the engineering journey—from research and planning to deployment and continuous improvement.

---

# 📖 My Story

My passion for technology didn't begin with programming.

It began with **curiosity**.

Growing up, my father was among the first people to introduce a computer to our city. Watching technology transform the way people worked sparked something in me that has never disappeared.

I wasn't interested in computers simply because they were machines.

I wanted to understand **how they worked**.

Every new component, every operating system, every line of code, every piece of hardware became another puzzle waiting to be explored.

That curiosity eventually became an obsession with learning.

Today, whether I'm working with AI models, backend architectures, frontend frameworks, distributed systems, or cloud infrastructure, that same curiosity continues to drive me forward.

---

# 💭 Why I Build Products

Many developers enjoy writing code.

I enjoy **building complete systems**.

I don't see programming as the final goal.

Programming is simply one tool that enables me to build products capable of solving real-world problems.

For me, the most rewarding part of engineering is watching an idea evolve into something people can genuinely use.

That is why I believe in:

- Building products instead of projects
- Solving problems instead of chasing trends
- Engineering for scalability instead of demonstrations
- Designing for long-term impact instead of short-term success
